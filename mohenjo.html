Mohenjo-daro & Harappan Civilization

Time Period:

The civilization thrived around 3300 BCE to 1300 BCE. The peak of the civilization is believed to have occurred around 2500 BCE.

Location:

  • It was one of the major cities of the Indus Valley Civilization alongside Mohenjo-Daro.
  • Harappa was located in present-day Pakistan, specifically in the Punjab region.

Architecture:

  • Buildings: Structures were made of brick, and many buildings were multi-storied.
  • Harappa had large granaries, public baths (like the Great Bath at Mohenjo-Daro), and warehouses for storing goods.
  • The Great Granary was believed to have been used for storing surplus grain.

The Harappan Civilization left a profound legacy, especially in terms of urban planning, trade, and craftsmanship. Some elements of Harappan culture, such as urban sanitation and craftsmanship, influenced subsequent civilizations in the Indian subcontinent.
